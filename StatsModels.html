Basic Statistical Models

Linear Models         Linear Mixed Models         Generalized Linear Models
Generalized Linear Mixed Models         Survival Models         Non-linear Mixed Model

Some basic type of statistical models are described below. For further details, please click the link above.

Linear Models:

Linear models (LM) are used to fit independent normal distributed response data (continuous data). It includes ANOVA models, ANCOVA models, simple linear regression, multiple linear regression, polynomial regression, etc. It models the mean with the characteristics (covariates, independent variables) of the individual subjects.

Linear Mixed Models:

Linear Mixed Models (LMM) are used to fit correlated normal distributed response data. It models the mean in a relationship with the characteristics (covariates) of individual subjects. It also models the variance-covariance matrix. The models can include fixed effects and random effects. LMM is a generalization of LM. It relaxes the assumptions of independence and common standand deviation from the LM.

Generalized Linear Models:

Generalized Linear models (GLM) are used to fit independent exponential family (normal and non-normal) distributed response data. It includes logistic regression model for binary data and Poisson models for count data, etc. It models the mean through a link function of the mean in a relationship with the characteristics (covariates) of the individual subjects. It is a generalization of the linear models (LM). It extends the normal distributed data to non-normal distributed data and continuous data to binary data, categorical data, and count data.

Generalized Linear Mixed Models:

Generalized Linear Mixed Models (GLMM) are used to fit correlated, exponential family (normal and non-normal) distributed response data. It models the mean via a link function of the mean in a relationship with the characteristics (covariates) of individual subjects. It also models the variance-covariance matrix. The models can include fixed effects and random effects. GLMM is a generalization of LM and GLM. It relaxes the independence assumption in the GLM and the normal assumption in LMM.

Note that the word "linear" means linear in model parameters! It does not mean linear in covariates (though it is frequently the case).

Survival Models:

Survival models are used to fit censored data. If the measurement is time to event, the data often contain censored data. The most common statistical methods used for such type of data are parametric regresson models, semi-parametric models (Cox proportional hazard models), and non-parametric models.

Non-Linear Mixed Models:

Sometimes the linear models described above may not fit the data well, in that case, a non-linear model may be needed. Non-linear models can be fitted to independent data (Non-linear models) or correlated data (Non-Linear Mixed Models). The distribution can be normal or non-normal.
